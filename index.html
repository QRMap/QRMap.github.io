QRMap: Real-time Online Open-set Queryable 3D Scene Reconstruction

Under Anonymous Review

QRMap builds 3D queryable map that provides spatial understanding of the environment in real-time.

Abstract

3D environmental mapping is crucial for embodied agents to interact with the physical world. Recent methods are either dependent on predefined concepts or are offline mapping, involving resource demanding large-scale foundation models to process the semantics. This paper introduces QRMap, Query-to-Region Map, a novel method for real-time online 3D mapping using RGB-D data that supports open-vocabulary queries. QRMap features in region-based semantic embedding, which holds superiority in extensibility to concurrent works that produce maps with point-based embedding. Notably, QRMap addresses the limitation of region-based embedding and extends to real-time incremental semantic mapping by combining it with he Truncated Signed Distance Function (TSDF). Impressively, QRMap achieves high-quality 3D segmentation in zero-shot manner showcasing the generalizability of the method, without the necessity for extensive 3D training or fine-tuning.

Approach

QRMap builds an open-vocabulary 3D queryable scene from a sequence of posed RGB-D images.
